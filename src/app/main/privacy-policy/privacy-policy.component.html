Privacy Policy

This privacy policy provides information on the collection, use, sharing etc. as of personal information by SOL, its affiliates and branch offices, holding Company, fellow subsidiaries/associates (collectively referred to as SOL) of its employees (current or former employees, trainees), customers, partners, contractors, vendors, current and former members of Board of Directors.

Further, while interacting with our websites, you also have the ability to link or connect with non-SOL websites. If you access those links through SOL website, you will leave the SOL website. SOL does not control those sites or their privacy practices, which may differ from SOL website. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of these parties before using any features. SOL would not be responsible for the usage of your personal data or any information provided to or collected by these websites.


How We Collect Your Personal Information:

We may collect the following information relating to you:

Offline Information is obtained during the course of interactions with you during in-person meetings, when you are in the process of purchase of any product, during conferences or gatherings or telephonic interactions;


Online Information originates from your activities on our website.


The websites automatically collect certain technical information relating to the device you use to visit the websites. Specifically, we use cookies, and similar technologies to help us understand your activity on the websites. We use cookies to personalize content and ads, to provide social media features and to analyze our traffic. A “cookie” is a small piece of data that a website can send to your browser, which may then be stored on your device. Data that we collect automatically includes your internet protocol (“IP”) address, location, your device’s identification information. For further information on the cookies and similar technologies we use on our websites and why we use them, please review our Cookie Policy.


Information that is collected from third party source: You may engage yourself through social media websites such as LinkedIn. At the time of engagement, you may allow us to have access to certain information from your social media profile based upon your privacy preference settings on such platforms.


Specific pieces of information about you that SOL may collect and process includes:

  • Name and physical address, email addresses, and telephone/mobile numbers;
  • Company data such as the name, designation, address, phone, fax, email;
  • Transactional data, including products and services ordered and payment methods;
  • Group name, department, organization name, city, URL

If you provide us with personal information of third parties (such as the details of your administrative assistant or your emergency contact), you agree to: (a) notify each individual before sharing their personal information with us, (b) explain that their personal information will be processed in accordance with this Privacy Notice, and (c) obtain their consent, where appropriate. SOL will not be responsible if the concerned third parties are not informed about the usage of their personal information.


How Do we Use Your Personal Information:

The personal information we collect may be used to:


  • Provide information and services as requested by you.
  • Provide better services and generate demand
  • Access website performance and usage analysis.
  • Complay with applicable laws and regulations and to operate our business.
  • Delivering information on services and products provided by each group company, or on the hosting of events and campaigns.

  • Market products and services based on legitimate business interest under the applicable law.

With your consent, we may also use your personal information for additional purposes. Instances where sensitive personal information is collected will warrant explicit consent and SOL will take the necessary steps to inform you of the same as may be required. Unless it is necessary for our legitimate business purposes, should you choose not to provide any personal information during certain activity, you will nevertheless be able to continue with such activity wherever possible.


International Transfers of Your Personal Information:

While we have distinct legal entities i.e. branches, group Companies in many parts of the world, SOL may transfer certain personal information across geographical borders to other entities or service providers in other countries working on our behalf in accordance with applicable law. For instance, data protection law of European Economic Area (EEA) authorizes transfer of personal information:


  • To countries which have been recognized by the European Commission as offering an adequate level of protection of personal information.
  • If appropriate measures in compliance with the European Union data protection legal requirements are in place in order to ensure the protection and confidentiality of personal information in the countries that does not offer an adequate level of protection.

The level of protection for personal information is not the same in all countries. However, we will take reasonable steps and implement measures as described in this Policy to keep your personal information secure.


SOL may also transfer your personal information to a third party located outside the EEA or Japan without having to implement the above measures where any of the following conditions are met:


  • Where consent has been given by the Data subject regarding transfer of his/her personal information.
  • The information which is to be transferred is available in public domain
  • Where transfer of personal information is necessary to protect the vital interest of the Data subject (to protect the life, body or property of data subject).
  • Where it is necessary for SOL to establish exercise or defend a legal claim.
  • Where it is in public interest.
  • Where it is required to improve public health or for promotion of health growth of children.

Sharing of Information with Third Parties:

We will not sell your personal information to anyone outside the SOL group companies unless we have obtain your consent. However, we may need to disclose your personal information to third parties in the following instances:


  • We may share your personal information by other companies within SOL group, which will be used in the manner as set out in privacy policy.
  • We may share information with statutory authorities, government institutions or other bodies where required for compliance with legal requirements.
  • We may disclose your personal information to our business partners if it is necessary for providing products and services to you, such as for certain software and related services.
  • If we make a sale or transfer of assets, or are otherwise involved in a merger or business/asset transfer, we may transfer your personal information to one or more third parties as part of that transaction.
  • We may share information where we believe it is necessary in order to investigate, prevent, or take action against any illegal activities, suspected fraud, situations involving potential threats to the physical safety of any person, or as otherwise required by law.
  • We may disclose the information to third parties only for legitimate purposes such as our service providers and agents for processing of data.

Data Protection and Confidentiality:

When third parties are given access to personal information, SOL will take appropriate contractual, technical and organizational measures designed to ensure that personal information is processed only to the extent that such processing is necessary, consistent with this Privacy Policy, and in accordance with applicable laws.


SOL will take all the reasonable steps to ensure that the information:


  • Is processed in fair manner.
  • Is obtained with the consent of the information provider.
